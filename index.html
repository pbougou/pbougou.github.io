About me

I' m a functional programmer interested in programming languages research. My research
interests lie in functional programming, type systems, compilers, and program verification.

Currently, I' m completing my thesis in Tail-calls in lazy functional languages at Software Engineering Laboratory at National Technical University of Athens under the supervision
of Prof. Nikolaos Papaspyrou.

As a student, I implemented software for many projects alone or with my fellow students.
These include, but not limited to, a full–stack web application — called FunSociety — for
Software Engineering course, in a group of 7 students, a compiler for Dana, an imperative
Turing–complete programming language for Compilers course, a virtual–machine for an
esoteric programming language — Befunge93, and type inference a la Hidney-Milner for
the simply-typed lambda calculus.

In my spare time, I enjoy listening to music and watching movies. Also, I love hiking, even
more when this time is shared with my dog, Oliver, who is truthfully full of kindness!
